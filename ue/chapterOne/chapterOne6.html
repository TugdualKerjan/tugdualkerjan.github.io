CLINT HAS HAD ENOUGH.

Clint Eastwood as a swiss bank worker in a suit carrying a light and portable briefcase in his right hand taking the metro back home through Lausanne at the end of the day --style raw

Clint the coworker takes the metro every day to get to work. The rhythmic clatter of the metro on the tracks, opening and closing of the doors, and the occasional screech of brakes hinder the experience of the ride. Overhead announcements, only auditive, providing information about the next stop might not be heard over his airpods. Certain passengers talk loud so as to hear each other over the noise to the annoyance of their neighboring travellers. Tourists, proffessionals and students crowd the metro at peak hours rendering it hard to find a place to sit down and start work early. The windows often give way to the darkness of the tunnel. The interior remains clean and dry as well as absent of signs of vandalism.

Clint Eastwood as a swiss bank worker in a suit struggling to have a phone call in a crowded train --style raw

Our persona is constantly working to provide for his family, even while going to and coming back from work. He constantly takes Lausanne's M2 to go to his different meetings, lunch with clients or fitness at the end of the day, working from his phone any time he's in the subway. The metro is a type of transportation that is cosmopolitan and open. Our persona wishes to make and answer phone calls in the metro but unfortunately can't find a silent place to do it. The people that surround him are strangers and he fails to see the value in exchanging with them for such a short period of time. Social norms allow him to work on his phone instead of interacting with his environment

Our unique system within luxury stores, enables individuals to donate their old electronic devices to designated collection boxes. In return , users can receive exclusive discount coupons. The next step is dismantling old batteries and PCB boards from these electronic devices. Rather than throwing them to waste, we've stitch them into the creation of luxurious handbags. These upcycled handbags are then made available to buy. In essence, our concept redefinines luxury, emphasizing the societal and environmental consequences of overconsumption by the wealthy.

Sketch Completion

Many white-collar workers are troubled by the tedious task of writing daily reports during their work. As a result, they have integrated the increasingly mature large language model into their daily lives, using AI to help them refine and write. A similar situation also arises among architects, who spend a significant amount of time on mundane details when completing their designs. Therefore, we hope to enable AI to assist architects in completing sketches competition. Architects can leverage AI to streamline modeling by providing basic ideas and 2D sketches. Even with minimal descriptions, like "a 1-meter-wide window on the kitchen wall," AI can complete the modeling process. This integration has the potential to enhance efficiency and accuracy in architecture.

Selected Project

Many buildings often face the challenge of not having precise plans for their interior structure and their integrated building systems, which are crucial for architects to efficiently redesign a project and avoid unexpected issues during construction. To streamline this process, X-ray technology can be harnessed for quality assurance to expedite the conversion of plans into CAD software, complete with detailed dimensions and material specifications. This innovation can significantly enhance the architectural redesign process, minimizing uncertainties and enhancing overall project efficiency.

Relevance and Impact

X-ray scanning houses has a big impact on architects and everyone involved in construction. It helps avoid mistakes by revealing hidden plumbing and electrical systems, reducing errors during planning and construction. Using 3D models from X-ray scans on-site also speeds up building, saving time and money.

STRAY DOGS!

In Chisinau, Moldova, the prevalence of stray dogs is a pressing issue. These resilient animals navigate the city's streets, facing challenges like hunger and disease due to the lack of a comprehensive municipal strategy. Despite sporadic individual efforts to feed and care for them, the number of strays overwhelms these initiatives. The absence of larger animal control measures and economic constraints exacerbate the problem. The situation underscores the need for a balanced approach, combining humane treatment with effective population control. While some organisations advocate for spaying and neutering programs, limited funding poses a significant obstacle. Doctor Vet Moldova, a local animal welfare organisation, strives to implement a long-term, sustainable, and strategic dog population management programme, using the CNVR approach (Catch – Neuter – Vaccinate – Return). Stray dogs in Chisinau serve as a visible manifestation of broader societal challenges, highlighting the importance of collaborative efforts to ensure the well-being of both the community and its canine inhabitants. Chisinau has a mix of modern style flat roofs and historial buildings - old wooden buildings and those abandoned soviet era buildings. In recent years, many of these historial buildings have become abandonned due to a downturned economy.

TO FEEL WANTED - DOG GUIDES

In Chisinau, a significant number of stray dogs face severe loneliness and lack essential social interaction, leading to distressing emotional and behavioral issues. The proposed solution is a unique program where stray dogs are trained to become interactive guides at key Soviet-era buildings in Chisinau. This small, dedicated spaces within these historic sites where people can visit, interact with the dogs along the tour. This approach serves a dual purpose: it provides much-needed companionship for the dogs and offers a unique engaging historical experience for visitors. This idea not only honors the historical significance of Chisinau's Soviet-era architecture but also adds a new dimension of community engagement and animal welfare. It aims to strengthen the bond between residents and stray dogs, fostering a sense of care, companionship, and shared history.

TO STAY SAFE - SMART ANIMAL CROSSINGS

Stray dogs in Chisinau face increased dangers due to heavy traffic, resulting in accidents and injuries. The absence of infrastructure tailored to their safety exacerbates the vulnerability of these animals in the city's bustling environment. Implementing smart animal-safe crossings equipped with infrared sensors in key areas of Chisinau directly addresses the city's unique challenge. These crossings offer real-time detection of stray dogs like Shadow, ensuring adaptive traffic signals and visible alerts for enhanced safety. In Chisinau, where stray dog populations are notable, the introduction of these crossings holds particular significance. It reflects a proactive approach to animal welfare within the city's urban fabric, demonstrating a commitment to the safety and well-being of both residents and the vulnerable stray dog community.

TO KEEP WARM - SKY PARKS

Chisinau's stray dogs struggle to find shelter during the city's cold winters, leading to health risks and disruption of the dogs in urban areas. The solution involves transforming Chisinau's flat rooftops into insulated, heated shelters for stray dogs. These rooftop refuges will be easily accessible and serve as safe havens during winter. To engage the community and enhance urban aesthetics, these shelters will feature art installations or murals and incorporate green spaces for natural insulation and exploration. This concept merges well with Chisinau's mix of historic and modern architecture. The shelters, designed to complement the city's look, use sustainable materials and green elements, supporting environmental goals. This initiative turns underused spaces into beneficial areas for strays, improving the urban landscape and fostering community care for animals.
